Biography of Niko Price

Niko Price, born on September 29, 1989, is an American mixed martial artist competing in the Welterweight division of the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC). Hailing from Cape Coral, Florida, Price developed his athletic prowess at Mariner High School, where he played linebacker for the football team.

After graduation, he delved into kickboxing and achieved two knockout victories. While briefly attending college to study criminal justice, Price began training in mixed martial arts in 2008.

Price embarked on his professional MMA career in February 2012 after an undefeated amateur run of 9-0. Prior to joining the UFC, he amassed an impressive record of 8-0, with seven wins by stoppage. His debut in the promotion took place at UFC 207 against Brandon Thatch, where he secured a first-round submission victory.

Throughout his UFC journey, Price encountered notable opponents such as Alex Morono, Alan Jouban, Vicente Luque, Randy Brown, Abdul Razak Alhassan, Tim Means, Geoff Neal, James Vick, Donald Cerrone, Michel Pereira, Robbie Lawler, and others.

He showcased his skills with memorable wins, including knockouts, submissions, and impressive performances that earned him multiple Performance of the Night bonuses.

Outside of the octagon, Niko Price leads a fulfilling personal life alongside his wife Erica, rais ing their six children. His dedication and achievements in the UFC have solidified his position as a respected welterweight competitor.
